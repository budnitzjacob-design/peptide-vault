PEPTIDE 1: BPC-157
================================================================================
Source URL: https://peptpedia.org/peptide/bpc-157

NAMES
  Primary:     BPC-157
  Scientific:  Body Protection Compound-157
  Proper:      Pentadecapeptide BPC 157
  CAS:         137525-51-0

MOLECULAR
  Formula:     C62H98N16O22
  MW:          1419.53 g/mol
  Sequence:    Gly-Glu-Pro-Pro-Pro-Gly-Lys-Pro-Ala-Asp-Asp-Ala-Gly-Leu-Val
  AA Count:    15
  Solubility:  Water soluble; reconstitute in bacteriostatic water

PHARMACOKINETICS
  Half-Life:   15.2 minutes (mean elimination; rats; IV)
  Clearance:   Undetectable within 4 hours post-administration
  Storage:     Lyophilized at -20°C; reconstituted at 4°C

DOSING
  Route              Dose                  Frequency
  -------            ----                  ---------
  Subcutaneous       10–20 mcg/kg          Once daily
  Intramuscular      10–20 mcg/kg          Once daily
  Intraperitoneal    10 mcg/kg – 10 mg/kg  Varies
  Oral (gavage)      10 mcg/kg – 10 mg/kg  Once daily
  Intravenous        10 mcg/kg             Single dose

MECHANISMS OF ACTION
  1. Growth Hormone Receptor (GHR) Upregulation — increases fibroblast sensitivity
  2. FAK-Paxillin Pathway — promotes cell adhesion and tissue repair
  3. Nitric Oxide Modulation — activates Src-Caveolin-1-eNOS phosphorylation
  4. Egr-1/NAB2 Gene Loop — "master switch and brake" for organized angiogenesis

PROTEIN CASCADES
  Growth:          GHR upregulation, fibroblast GH sensitivity
  Immune:          Reduces TNF-alpha, IL-1 beta, IL-6; enhances antioxidant systems
  Cytokines:       TNF-alpha ↓, IL-1β ↓, IL-6 ↓
  Proliferation:   Wound granulation, re-epithelialization
  Metabolism:      Nitric oxide system modulation
  Cancer:          Not primary; aberrant angiogenesis prevention via Egr-1/NAB2

RESEARCH EFFECTS
  Tissue Repair:    Skeletal muscle, tendon, ligament, bone — accelerated healing
  GI Protection:    NSAID ulcers, alcohol damage, IBD models, Crohn's models
  Anti-inflammatory: Reduces pro-inflammatory cytokines, ROS
  Wound Healing:    2-3x faster in various models vs controls
  Neuroprotection:  TBI protection, peripheral nerve regeneration
  Organoprotection: Cardiac arrhythmia, hepatic, renal, pulmonary models

HUMAN USE
  Clinical Trials:  None completed or published as of 2026
  Status:           Not approved for human use; research compound

ANIMAL MODELS
  Published Studies: >100 preclinical
  Species:          Primarily rat models

CITATIONS
  [1] Sikiric et al. (2011). Stable gastric pentadecapeptide BPC 157 in trials for
      inflammatory bowel disease. Current Pharmaceutical Design.
  [2] Chang et al. (2014). Pentadecapeptide BPC 157 enhances the growth hormone
      receptor expression in tendon fibroblasts. Molecules.
  [3] Seveljevic-Jaran et al. (2018). BPC 157 and standard angiogenic growth
      factors. Journal of Physiology and Pharmacology.
  [4] Hsieh et al. (2020). Modulatory effects of BPC 157 on vasomotor tone.
      Scientific Reports.
  [5] Seiwerth et al. (2021). Stable Gastric Pentadecapeptide BPC 157 and Wound
      Healing. Frontiers in Pharmacology.

CATEGORIES
  Recovery & Repair

SAFETY PROFILE
  Safety:       No serious adverse events in animal studies; no human data
  Rating:       Preclinical safety established; human risk unknown
